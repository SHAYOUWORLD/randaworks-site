Overview

Why this game feels different

Real history becomes system logic

The order of events matters. When you understand why one event leads to another, your deck starts producing stronger turns.

Learning is tied to strategy

This is not history pasted onto a card game. The historical chain itself is part of the strategy and reward loop.

Short demo, clear hook

The current demo is built to quickly show the core loop: connect historical causality, trigger stronger combos, and push deeper.

Cause-and-effect combos

A sample combo chain

Battle of Okehazama → Battle of Nagashino → Honno-ji Incident

When the flow of history lines up, the chain becomes a strong Sengoku route combo.

Game flow

What a run looks like

1. Build your route

Pick cards and build around historical chains that can keep scaling.

2. Trigger linked events

Line up the order correctly and the board state starts rewarding historical logic.

3. Push deeper with better understanding

The more the structure of history makes sense to you, the more strategic options open up.
