Why

Why this approach matters to us

We are not trying to make “educational games” in the usual sense. The goal is not to teach first and entertain second.

Instead, we want to build experiences where the player feels the structure, notices a pattern, and suddenly understands why something works. That moment of understanding should be satisfying on its own.

Japanese history is simply the first arena where that design philosophy becomes visible.

How We Think

Design principles

Understanding should be actionable

Insight should lead to better decisions, stronger runs, and cleaner strategies.

Discovery should feel rewarding

The player should feel a real payoff when the system clicks into place.

Teaching should be invisible

The experience comes first. If learning happens, it should happen through play.

Keep the core sharp

As a small team, we prefer strong fundamentals over feature sprawl.
